Brian Ricks is an Assistant Professor of Instruction in the Computer Science Department at the University of Texas at Dallas (UTD). He holds a PhD, MS, and BS from UTD, specializing in wireless networking, network security, and Bayesian networks. His academic journey was shaped by the guidance of leading experts Bhavani Thuraisingham, Sriraam Natarajan, and Patrick Tague, who all co-advised his doctoral work.

Brian was a member of the MEWS research group at Carnegie Mellon University: Silicon Valley (CMU-SV), where he focused on the simulation of networks and security solutions.

Brian is deeply passionate about education, but when he is not busy teaching, Brian enjoys video game design, working on car audio systems, staying active with regular workouts, and mentoring students. He is also well known for his enthusiasm for free food!
